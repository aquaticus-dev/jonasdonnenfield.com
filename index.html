About

I am a Predoctoral Research Associate and PhD student in the UW School of Oceanography (expected June 2027), advised by Dr. Maureen H. Walczak. My work uses marine sediment geochemistry to reconstruct past ocean chemistry, climate, and ice-sheet change across the Northeast Pacific, Greenland, and Baltic Sea.

I develop and apply techniques including LA-ICP-MS, synchrotron imaging, and multiproxy signal extraction to recover robust paleoceanographic signals from complex sediment records. I am especially interested in how these archives constrain ocean oxygenation, deglacial change, carbon cycling, and the environmental context for future climate solutions.

I received my B.A. in Geology from Carleton College (Northfield, MN), where I worked with Dr. Tessa M. Hill at UC Davis' Bodega Marine Laboratory on benthic foraminifera and coastal ocean geochemistry.
