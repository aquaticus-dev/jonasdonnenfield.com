Research

My research uses marine sediment archives to reconstruct how ocean chemistry, climate, ice sheets, and carbon cycling have changed across glacial-interglacial timescales. I work across Northeast Pacific continental margin records, Greenland and Baltic Sea deglacial sediments, and proxy-development problems where diagenesis, variable sedimentation, and mixed source signals complicate interpretation. Across these projects, I develop and apply analytical approaches—including laser ablation mass spectrometry, synchrotron imaging, and statistical signal extraction—to recover reliable records of past environmental change.

Pacific Northwest Hydroclimate & Columbia River Discharge

Read project overview

My MS and current PhD research reconstructs ~12,000 years of Pacific Northwest climate variability and Columbia River discharge history using sediment cores from the NE Pacific continental margin. Using organic biomarkers (alkenones, GDGTs), foraminiferal Mg/Ca, benthic assemblages, and sediment physical properties, I disentangle the competing influences of river input, marine productivity, and early diagenesis. This work helps constrain how the Pacific Northwest hydrological cycle responded to past climate forcing and provides context for future change.

NE Pacific Benthic Hypoxia & Oxygen Minimum Zone Dynamics

Read project overview

The Pacific Northwest continental margin hosts one of the most intense oxygen minimum zones in the modern ocean. Using benthic foraminifera as low-oxygen proxies, alongside geochemical indicators, I reconstruct the history of bottom-water deoxygenation from the late Pleistocene through the Holocene. A central goal is testing whether persistent hypoxia was driven by Columbia River freshwater discharge, marine productivity, or large-scale Pacific circulation.

Proxy Development in Complex Continental Margin Settings

Read project overview

Continental margin sediments offer high-resolution climate records but present challenges for proxy interpretation: diagenetic alteration of foraminiferal calcite, mixed terrestrial and marine organic matter, and rapidly varying sedimentation rates. I use LA-ICP-MS trace element mapping, synchrotron micro-CT scanning, and statistical approaches to assess diagenetic overprinting and establish reliable paleoclimate signals from complex archives.

Ice Sheet Deglaciation & Meltwater Dynamics

Read project overview

As part of the Baffin Bay Deglacial Experiment (BADEX), I contributed to research on the spatiotemporal demise of the Greenland Ice Sheet using sediment cores from west Greenland. Through collaboration with Lund University, I also contributed to work on Fennoscandian Ice Sheet meltwater release and potential links with Heinrich Stadial 1.

Ocean Carbon Cycle & Paleo Constraints

Read project overview

A growing direction in my work is connecting paleoceanographic records with questions in ocean carbon cycling and climate solutions. Marine sediments preserve evidence for how circulation, oxygenation, terrestrial inputs, and carbonate chemistry shape carbon storage over long timescales. I am interested in applying that perspective to emerging climate-solutions questions, including marine carbon dioxide removal (mCDR), where proxy constraints can help evaluate durability, monitoring, and ecosystem context.
